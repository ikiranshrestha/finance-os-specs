# Project Charter

**Project:** FinanceOS

**Version:** 0.1.0 (Draft)

**Status:** Draft

---

# Purpose

FinanceOS exists to provide a reliable, transparent, and extensible platform for managing personal finances.

The project aims to simplify financial management by combining budgeting, expense tracking, savings planning, investment tracking, financial insights, and future integrations into a single cohesive platform.

FinanceOS is designed as a long-term platform rather than a collection of disconnected features. Every capability should contribute to helping users understand, manage, and improve their financial lives.

---

# Vision

To build a modern, offline-first personal finance platform that is trustworthy, maintainable, and adaptable to changing financial needs.

FinanceOS should empower users to make informed financial decisions through accurate data, clear organization, and thoughtful design.

---

# Mission

The mission of FinanceOS is to create a finance platform that:

* Organizes financial information in a structured and meaningful way.
* Helps users understand where their money comes from and where it goes.
* Encourages responsible financial habits through planning and visibility.
* Supports future growth without requiring major architectural redesign.
* Remains reliable regardless of internet connectivity.

---

# Scope

The initial scope of FinanceOS includes:

* Personal account management.
* Income and expense tracking.
* Categories and budgets.
* Savings and financial goals.
* Financial reporting and dashboards.
* Offline-first operation.
* Cross-platform support through Flutter.

Future versions may include:

* Cloud synchronization.
* Multi-device support.
* Shared finance.
* Investment portfolio tracking.
* Loan management.
* Tax planning.
* Bank integrations.
* AI-assisted financial insights.

---

# Guiding Principles

FinanceOS is guided by the following principles:

* Offline-first by design.
* User data belongs to the user.
* Simplicity over unnecessary complexity.
* Specification before implementation.
* Modular architecture.
* Long-term maintainability.
* Transparency in financial calculations.
* Security and privacy by default.

---

# Success Criteria

FinanceOS will be considered successful when it:

* Provides a dependable personal finance experience.
* Maintains a clear separation between business rules and implementation.
* Can evolve without breaking existing functionality.
* Supports future expansion while preserving architectural consistency.
* Serves as a long-term foundation for additional financial capabilities.

---

# Out of Scope

The following are intentionally excluded from the initial release:

* Multi-user collaboration.
* Enterprise financial management.
* Mandatory cloud dependency.
* Bank integrations.
* AI-driven financial automation.
* Cryptocurrency trading.
* Stock trading.

These may be introduced in future versions after the core platform is mature.

---

# Governance

This Project Charter serves as the foundational document for FinanceOS.

All future specifications, architectural decisions, requirements, and implementations should align with the vision, mission, and principles established in this document.

Changes to this charter should be made deliberately, reviewed carefully, and versioned appropriately.
